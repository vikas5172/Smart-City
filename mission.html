Mission
Smart Cities Mission was launched by the Hon’ Prime Minister on 25 June, 2015. The main objective of the Mission is to promote cities that provide core infrastructure, clean and sustainable environment and give a decent quality of life to their citizens through the application of ‘smart solutions’. The Mission aims to drive economic growth and improve quality of life through comprehensive work on social, economic, physical and institutional pillars of the city. The focus is on sustainable and inclusive development by creation of replicable models which act as lighthouses to other aspiring cities. 100 cities have been selected to be developed as Smart Cities through a two-stage competition.

The Mission is operated as a Centrally Sponsored Scheme. Central Government will give financial support to the extent of Rs. 48,000 crores over 5 years i.e. on an average Rs.100 crore per city per year. An equal amount on a matching basis is to be provided by the State/ULB. Additional resources are to be raised through convergence, from ULBs’ own funds, grants under Finance Commission, innovative finance mechanisms such as Municipal Bonds, other government programs and borrowings. Emphasis has been given on the participation of private sector through Public Private Partnerships (PPP). Citizens’ aspirations were captured in the Smart City Proposals (SCPs) prepared by the selected cities. Aggregated at the national level, these proposals contained more than 5,000 projects worth over Rs. 2,00,000 crores, of which 45 percent is to be funded through Mission grants, 21 percent through convergence, 21 percent through PPP and rest from other sources.

Dholera Smart City Project


What is Dholera Smart City?
Dholera is an upcoming smart city located in the Ahmedabad District of Gujarat, India. When completed, it will be one of the largest smart cities in the country and a model for other major cities in India. The city is being developed by the Dholera SIR, a Special Investment Region (SIR) developed by the Gujarat government. Dholera SIR is expected to contribute $110 billion to the Indian economy and create 1.2 million jobs by 2040. The city will be developed over 4,500 acres of land. Once completed, the city will have a population of 1 million people. The city is expected to be completed by 2040.

Timeline for Dholera Smart City

The Gujarat government has been considering the development of Dholera as a new city since 2005. However, it wasn’t until 2011 that the government began actively pursuing the city’s development. The government announced their decision to develop Dholera as a Special Investment Region (SIR) in 2013, and the city was officially announced as a smart city in 2016. The first construction phase is expected to finish by 2022, and the project is expected to be completed by 2040.

Amravati Smart City Project

What is Amravati Smart City?
Amaravati has been selected in Round 3 of the Smart City Challenge (June 2017). As per the Smart Cities Mission Guidelines, a Special Purpose Vehicle is required to be established by each selected smart city with a Managing Team and apprise the Ministry about the action taken regularly. Accordingly, a SPV has been incorporated in the name and style of “Amaravati Smart & Sustainable City Corporation Limited” on 21.11.2017 by the Ministry of Corporate Affairs.The projects proposed under the Amaravati Smart Cities Mission are dovetailed with the ongoing projects being executed by

APCRDA under Amaravati Capital City construction works and are aimed to develop Amaravati as an innovative and eco-friendly city with self-sustaining infrastructure and natural ecosystems that uses ICTs to improve quality of life, the efficiency of urban operations and services and competitiveness, while ensuring that it meets the needs of present and future generations with respect to economic, social, environmental and cultural aspects.

Dehradun Smart City Project

What is Dehradun Smart

City?

Dehradun- the capital of Uttarakhand- is among the oldest cities of India, situated in the foothills of the Himalayas and Shivalik mountain region. The city is also known as Doon, meaning valley, which houses many tourist attractions like Barkot temple, Robbers Cave, Clement Town, etc. To make the city more liveable for the citizens and promote tourism, the city was listed in
the Smart City Mission in phase 4 by the Government of India. Dehradun was declared a part of the Smart City Mission project in Phase 4 and was one of the 100 smart cities selected by the government of India. The city was included in the list in 2017, and the foundation day of the Smart City Mission was held on 25 June 2019 by the former Chief Minister of Uttarakhand, Trivendra Singh Rawat. The Smart City Mission is a bold and new initiative of the government of India which aims to improve the living quality of the citizens of India.


Keypoints

Provide citizens with application solutions that are smart, such as e-governance and tax payment systems.
Ensure that the city is developed sustainably. 10 out of the 60 wards in Dehradun are covered by the Smart City Mission.
